# 1. Introduction

AstraNova Global has experienced rapid business growth across multiple regions and departments. As the organization expanded, each department adopted separate operational systems to manage its own data.

Although these systems support day-to-day operations, they do not provide a unified enterprise-wide view of business performance. Executives often rely on manually prepared reports from different departments, resulting in delayed decisions and inconsistent metrics.

HELIX is proposed as the organization's centralized Enterprise Data Intelligence Platform to integrate data, standardize KPIs, automate reporting, and enable intelligent business decision-making.

---

# 2. Business Challenges

The organization currently faces the following challenges:

## Sales

- Revenue reports are delayed.
- Regional sales comparisons require manual effort.
- Product performance is difficult to analyze across markets.
- Sales forecasting lacks consistency.

---

## Finance

- Budget tracking is maintained manually.
- Profitability analysis takes significant time.
- Financial KPIs differ across departments.
- Executive financial reporting is not automated.

---

## Supply Chain

- Inventory shortages occur unexpectedly.
- Supplier performance is difficult to monitor.
- Warehouse stock visibility is limited.
- Demand forecasting is inaccurate.
- Stock replenishment is reactive instead of proactive.

---

## Marketing

- Campaign ROI is difficult to measure.
- Customer acquisition costs vary significantly.
- Marketing channels cannot be compared effectively.
- Customer journey visibility is incomplete.

---

## Human Resources

- Employee attrition trends are not monitored proactively.
- Recruitment performance is difficult to evaluate.
- Workforce planning relies on manual spreadsheets.
- Training effectiveness cannot be measured.

---

## Customer Support

- Ticket resolution times vary across regions.
- Customer satisfaction trends are difficult to monitor.
- Escalated issues lack visibility.
- Support workload is unevenly distributed.

---

# 3. Business Goals

HELIX aims to achieve the following objectives:

- Create a single source of truth for enterprise data.
- Eliminate manual reporting processes.
- Standardize KPI definitions across departments.
- Improve operational efficiency.
- Enable real-time business monitoring.
- Support predictive analytics.
- Improve executive decision-making.
- Increase data quality and trust.

---

# 4. Functional Business Requirements

HELIX shall:

- Integrate data from multiple enterprise systems.
- Validate and clean incoming data automatically.
- Store data in a centralized warehouse.
- Generate standardized KPIs.
- Support historical trend analysis.
- Provide interactive dashboards.
- Deliver role-based reporting.
- Generate AI-powered business summaries.
- Detect anomalies in business performance.
- Support forecasting and planning.

---

# 5. Success Criteria

The implementation of HELIX will be considered successful if:

- Manual reporting effort is significantly reduced.
- Business users access consistent KPIs.
- Executive dashboards provide real-time visibility.
- Forecast accuracy improves over time.
- Data quality issues decrease.
- Decision-making becomes faster.
- Cross-department reporting becomes seamless.

---

# 6. Expected Business Benefits

HELIX is expected to deliver measurable value by:

- Reducing reporting time.
- Increasing operational transparency.
- Improving collaboration across departments.
- Enabling proactive decision-making.
- Supporting strategic planning.
- Enhancing customer satisfaction.
- Optimizing inventory management.
- Improving financial visibility.

---

# 7. Risks

Potential implementation risks include:

- Poor source data quality.
- Resistance to process changes.
- Inconsistent business definitions.
- Integration challenges between systems.
- Data governance issues.

---

# 8. Assumptions

The project assumes:

- Enterprise data sources are available.
- Departments agree on KPI definitions.
- Historical business data can be integrated.
- Business users will adopt standardized reporting.
- Leadership supports data-driven decision-making.

---

# 9. Project Scope

### Included

- Enterprise Data Warehouse
- ETL Pipelines
- Data Quality Framework
- Business Intelligence Dashboards
- KPI Engine
- Forecasting
- AI Insights
- Executive Reporting

### Excluded (Version 1)

- Live ERP Integration
- IoT Device Streaming
- Mobile Application
- Real-time Streaming Analytics
- Generative AI Chat Assistant

These capabilities may be considered in future versions.
